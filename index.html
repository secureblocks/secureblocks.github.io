SecureBlocks.

Secure Blocks (K-12) is an exciting incursion program that uses the vibrant world of Minecraft to teach cybersecurity in a way that's both fun and easy to grasp. In this hands-on experience, students embark on epic red team and blue team adventures, where they build, attack, and defend digital fortresses within the game. By transforming complex cybersecurity concepts into interactive Minecraft challenges, Secure Blocks turns learning about cyber threats and defences into an engaging and enjoyable activity. This innovative approach not only simplifies tricky ideas but also fosters creativity and teamwork, helping students become savvy cyber defenders, all while having a blast.

This is a Digital Playhouse Foundation Initiate, with proceeds going into the charity.

Password entropy is usually expressed in terms of bits. The lower the number of bits, the easier it is to guess the password. The higher the number, the more difficult it becomes to crack. An already-known password has 0 bits of entropy; one that can be guessed on the first attempt half the time has 1 bit of entropy.
